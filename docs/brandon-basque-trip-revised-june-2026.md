# Brandon's Basque Country Trip — REVISED (Slow Travel Edition)

**Dates:** Sat June 27 (arrive 6pm) - Sun July 5 (depart early AM)  
**Days:** 8 days, 7 nights  
**Vibe:** Slow travel, options not obligations, one surf session, pilgrim experience, young people scene  
**Transport:** Car rental, with flexibility for Camino walking + albergue stay

---

## 🎯 TRIP PHILOSOPHY

**Not a checklist, a framework:**
- Build in downtime and wandering
- Options for each day (not rigid plans)
- Meet locals and younger Basques
- Experience pilgrim life without car stress
- France border is easy (Schengen = no checkpoints)
- Surf once for the experience, not the main event

---

## 🗓️ FLEXIBLE ITINERARY

### **Day 1: Sat June 27 — Arrive & Settle**

**Arrival:**
- Fly in: Arrive Bilbao ~6pm
- Pick up rental car at airport
- Drive to accommodation (15 min)

**Evening (Low-Key):**
- Check in, decompress from travel
- Walk Casco Viejo (Old Town)
- Light pintxos dinner (not the big crawl — save that)
- Early night to recover

**Sleep:** Bilbao (Old Town area)

**Vibe:** Ease in, don't force it

---

### **Day 2: Sun June 28 — Bilbao Exploration**

**Morning:**
- Sleep in if needed
- Coffee + pastries in a local spot
- Wander Old Town markets (if Sunday market is on)

**Option A: Art/Culture**
- Guggenheim Museum (iconic building even if you skip inside)
- Walk the riverfront
- Explore different Bilbao neighborhoods

**Option B: Nature**
- Drive to **Mount Anboto** (45 min)
- Hike to Mari's cave (3-4 hours)
- Sacred site, mystical vibe
- Back to Bilbao evening

**Option C: Super Chill**
- Café hopping
- Bookstores, markets
- People watching
- No agenda

**Evening:**
- Pintxos crawl in Casco Viejo (THE experience)
- Bar hop: Gure Toki, El Globo, Sorginzulo
- Txikiteo (social bar hopping)

**Sleep:** Bilbao

**Drive time:** 0-1.5 hours (if you do Anboto)

---

### **Day 3: Mon June 29 — Camino + Pilgrim Experience**

**The Plan:**
- **Walk a Camino section** AND **stay in an albergue** (pilgrim hut)
- Logistics: Drive to starting point, walk to ending point with albergue, taxi back to car next morning

**Recommended Route:**
- **Bilbao (Portugalete) → Pobeña** (12-15 km, ~4 hours)
- Coastal Camino Norte
- Beautiful cliffs, beach sections
- End in Pobeña (or continue to Castro-Urdiales)

**Albergue Stay:**
- Book ahead: Albergue in Pobeña or Castro-Urdiales
- €10-15/night
- Communal sleeping, shared kitchen
- Meet other pilgrims (international, all ages)

**Car Logistics:**
- Park at starting point (Portugalete)
- Walk to albergue
- Next morning: taxi back to car (€15-20)
- OR arrange car drop-off service

**What You'll Experience:**
- Camino culture (pilgrims from everywhere)
- Simple living (bunk beds, shared meals)
- Evening pilgrim dinner/conversation
- Credential stamping (pilgrim passport)
- The "Camino vibe" without committing to weeks

**Sleep:** Albergue in Pobeña or Castro-Urdiales

**Drive time:** ~30 min to trailhead

---

### **Day 4: Tue June 30 — Coastal Villages + Young Scene**

**Morning:**
- Taxi back to car (or walk if close)
- Drive coastal route toward San Sebastián

**Stop 1: Gernika** (30 min from coast)
- Tree of Gernika (sacred oak)
- Monday market if timing works (if not, just the tree)
- Picasso's "Guernica" story
- 30-45 min visit

**Stop 2: Mundaka** (15 min from Gernika)
- Famous surf town
- **SURF SESSION HERE** (or Bakio nearby)
- Rent board (€15-20)
- Chill surf town vibe
- Lunch at local spot

**Afternoon Options:**

**Option A: More Coastal Villages**
- **Lekeitio** — authentic fishing town, beautiful
- **Elantxobe** — tiny cliffside village (insanely photogenic)
- Beach time, seafood lunch

**Option B: San Juan de Gaztelugatxe**
- Iconic island hermitage
- Game of Thrones filming location
- Short hike, dramatic views
- Spiritual site (built on pagan location)

**Evening:**
- Arrive **Zarautz** or **San Sebastián**
- Zarautz = surf town, younger vibe, quieter
- San Sebastián = city, more energy, nightlife

**Young People Scene:**
- Zarautz: surf hostels, beach bars, skater vibe
- San Sebastián Gros neighborhood: young professionals, surf culture

**Sleep:** Zarautz or San Sebastián

**Drive time:** ~2 hours total (with stops)

---

### **Day 5: Wed July 1 — San Sebastián Day**

**Morning (Flexible):**
- Sleep in or early beach walk
- Coffee in Old Town (Parte Vieja)

**Option A: Hike Mount Ulia**
- Start from Old Town
- Coastal trail, 2-3 hours round trip
- Panoramic views of La Concha Bay
- Easy-moderate

**Option B: Beach/Surf**
- Zurriola Beach (surf beach in Gros)
- Swim or rent board if you want second surf
- Just chill

**Option C: Wander**
- Explore neighborhoods
- San Sebastián is perfect for aimless walking
- Markets, shops, cafes

**Afternoon:**
- Beach time at La Concha
- Most beautiful city beach in Europe
- Chill, swim, read

**Evening:**
- **PINTXOS CRAWL** in Parte Vieja
- This is THE San Sebastián experience
- Bar hop: Gandarias, La Cuchara de San Telmo, Borda Berri
- Most Michelin stars per capita in the world
- Social, fun, iconic

**Night Option:**
- Gros neighborhood bars (younger crowd)
- Live music venues
- Beach bonfire if locals are doing it

**Sleep:** San Sebastián

**Drive time:** 0 (walking day)

---

### **Day 6: Thu July 2 — France Border Day**

**The Easy Border Crossing:**
- Schengen zone = **no border checks**
- Just drive across, no passport needed
- 15 min from San Sebastián

**Morning:**
- Drive to **Hondarribia** (30 min)
- Medieval walled town on French border
- Colorful Basque houses
- Wander old town, harbor
- Coffee with view of France across river

**Cross into France:**
- Drive 5 min to **Saint-Jean-de-Luz** (France)
- Charming French Basque town
- Different vibe, same culture
- Beach, markets, cafes

**Afternoon: Zugarramurdi Witches' Cave** (45 min inland)
- **This is a must-do**
- Massive cave where "witches" gathered
- 1610 witch trials site
- Museum about Basque witchcraft/mythology
- Spiritual, powerful, eerie
- Walk through the cave (free)
- Museum (€4)

**Alternative Afternoon:**
- Stay on French coast
- **Biarritz** (30 min from Saint-Jean-de-Luz)
- Surf town, belle époque architecture
- Young expat/surf scene
- Beach, cafes, nightlife

**Evening:**
- Return to San Sebastián OR
- Stay in French Basque Country
- Bayonne has great young scene (university town)

**Sleep:** San Sebastián or French side (Bayonne/Biarritz)

**Drive time:** ~2-3 hours total (with border crossing)

**This is your "choose your own adventure" day:**

**Option A: Deep Spiritual**

- Dramatic setting

**Option B: Surf Town Immersion**

**Option C: University Town**
- Drive to **Pamplona** (1.5 hours)
- University city, young energy
- Hemingway's "The Sun Also Rises" setting
- Beautiful old town
- Different vibe from coast

**Option D: Artist Community**
- **Oñati** (near Arantzazu)
- Historic university town
- Artist/creative community
- Gorgeous Basque architecture
- Very local, not touristy

**Option E: Chill & Integrate**
- Stay in San Sebastián
- No agenda
- Follow what feels good
- Beach, cafes, markets
- Process the trip

**Friday Night (Significant!):**
- **Friday is sacred in Basque mythology**
- Mari and Sugaar meet on Friday nights
- Akelarre (witch gathering) night
- If you're at sacred site, extra meaningful
- Or just dinner with intention

**Sleep:** San Sebastián or wherever you land

**Drive time:** Varies (0-3 hours)

## 🚗 CAR + CAMINO LOGISTICS

### **How to Do Albergue Stay with Car:**

**Method 1: Taxi Back (Easiest)**
- Day 1: Drive to trailhead, park car
- Walk to albergue
- Day 2: Taxi back to car in morning (€15-25)
- Continue trip
- **Apps:** Cabify, Free Now (Uber doesn't work well in rural areas)

**Method 2: Circular Route**
- Walk a loop that returns near car
- Stay at albergue along the way
- Example: Bilbao → Pobeña → Bilbao (different route back)

**Method 3: Arrange Car Pickup**
- Some albergues help coordinate
- Local taxi driver picks you up, brings you to car
- Or friend/Airbnb host helps

**Recommended for You:**
- **Bilbao/Portugalete → Pobeña** (one-way)
- Taxi back next morning
- Simple, authentic experience

## 🇫🇷 FRANCE BORDER DETAILS

**Super Easy:**
- **No passport control** (Schengen zone)
- Just drive across
- Signs change to French
- That's it

**What Changes:**
- Language (French Basque vs Spanish Basque)
- Currency (still Euro)
- Food (more French influence)
- Vibe (same culture, different country)

**Recommended Route:**
- San Sebastián → Hondarribia (30 min)
- Cross at Hendaye/Irun
- Saint-Jean-de-Luz → Biarritz → Bayonne
- Inland to Zugarramurdi
- Circle back

**French Basque Highlights:**
- **Saint-Jean-de-Luz** — charming port town
- **Biarritz** — surf, belle époque architecture
- **Bayonne** — chocolate, university town, old city
- **Zugarramurdi** — witches cave (technically Spain but near border)

**San Sebastián:**
- **Gros neighborhood** — surf culture, young professionals
- **Zurriola Beach** bars — sunset sessions
- **Parte Vieja** nightlife (but touristy)
- Coworking spaces / cafes (digital nomads)

**Bilbao:**
- **Bilbao La Vieja** — artist neighborhood, alternative scene
- **Casco Viejo** — student bars, cheap pintxos
- **Café Antzokia** — cultural center, live music
- Street art scene

**Zarautz:**
- **Surf hostels** — meet traveling surfers
- Beach bars / chiringuitos
- Skate park scene

**Bayonne (France):**
- **University town** — student energy
- River bars and cafes
- More alternative/artsy than touristy

**Mundaka:**
- **Surf community** — international + local
- Small, tight-knit

### **How to Connect:**

**Surf Shops/Hostels:**
- Ask about evening hangouts
- Group surf sessions
- Often organize dinners

**Markets:**
- Sunday markets = local scene
- Chat with vendors
- Younger folks selling art/crafts

**Coworking/Cafes:**
- Digital nomad hubs in San Sebastián
- Work-friendly cafes
- Strike up conversations

**Couchsurfing Hangouts:**
- Even if not staying with hosts
- "Hangouts" feature shows local meetups
- Language exchanges, hikes, dinners

**Albergues:**
- Other pilgrims = instant community
- Mix of ages, many young travelers
- Shared meals = natural conversation

**Ask Your Airbnb Host:**
- Where do young locals go?
- Live music? Events?
- Off-tourist-path spots
